Does Wayfair ship to South Africa?

The short answer is no — Wayfair doesn't deliver to South Africa directly. Here's the honest workaround if you really want that piece, and the cheaper local route most people should try first.

Wayfair is one of the world's biggest home and furniture stores, so it's no surprise South Africans want it. Unfortunately, Wayfair does not ship to South Africa. As of 2026, Wayfair sells and delivers only within a small set of markets — the United States, Canada, the United Kingdom, Germany and Ireland — and there's no South African delivery option at checkout.

So how do I get Wayfair items in SA?

The only reliable route is a freight forwarder. You sign up for a service that gives you a US or UK delivery address, Wayfair ships to that address, and the forwarder sends the parcel on to South Africa. South Africans commonly use Aramex Global Shopper (local and well-known), MyUS, and Shop&Ship.

Be realistic about the total, though. On top of Wayfair's price you'll pay the forwarder's fee, international freight (furniture is bulky and heavy), and — since the 2024 SARS change — customs duty plus 15% VAT on the declared value.1 For a large item, those costs add up fast.

⚠️ Do the full maths first. Freight on bulky homeware is where forwarding gets expensive. Add Wayfair's price + forwarding + freight + duty + VAT before you decide — for many items, a local SA store works out cheaper and arrives in days, not weeks.

A forwarder is best reserved for a specific item you genuinely can't source any other way.

Frequently asked questions

Does Wayfair ship to South Africa?

No. Wayfair does not deliver to South Africa. It ships only within a few markets such as the United States, Canada, the United Kingdom, Germany and Ireland, so there is no South African delivery option at checkout.

How can I buy from Wayfair in South Africa?

Use a freight forwarder such as Aramex Global Shopper, MyUS or Shop&Ship. You get a US or UK address, Wayfair ships there, and the forwarder sends it to South Africa. You pay the forwarding fee, international freight, and import duty plus 15% VAT.

Is it worth forwarding Wayfair furniture to SA?

Often not, because furniture is bulky and freight plus duties can exceed the item's price. Do the full landed-cost maths first, and compare against a local South African home store, which is usually cheaper and faster.

Are there local alternatives to Wayfair in South Africa?

Yes. Several South African home and decor retailers carry comparable furniture and homeware with direct local delivery. It's usually cheaper and quicker than forwarding a Wayfair order internationally.
