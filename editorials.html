ASOS in 2026: the only fast-fashion-adjacent site I still bother with

It used to be the default for anyone under 30 in the English-speaking world. Then quality dipped, Boohoo bought rivals, and Shein ate the bottom half of the market. So why is ASOS quietly the smartest place to buy clothes online in 2026? Three reasons most shoppers miss.

ASOS does one thing better than almost any apparel retailer on the planet — it carries roughly 850 brands under one checkout. That's not just convenience. It's a pricing arbitrage opportunity, because the same Levi's 501s, the same Adidas Sambas, the same Carhartt WIP beanie are all there, and ASOS routinely discounts third-party brands more aggressively than the brands' own websites.

The site has changed character in the last 24 months. After a brutal 2023 inventory writedown the company pivoted from "more SKUs" to "fewer, better." If you're shopping ASOS the way you did in 2019 — endless scroll through ASOS DESIGN basics — you're missing the new value. The interesting stock is the third-party brand catalogue: Weekday, COS, Stüssy, Reebok, Topshop (relaunched in 2023), New Balance, Salomon. Filter by brand, ignore the house line, and you're shopping a mid-tier department store with weekly discounts.

Where ASOS beats every alternative

Returns. Free returns for Premier members in 30+ countries. No "store credit only," no restocking fees on full-price items. If you order three sizes of the same jeans to find the right one, two go back with no friction. This is the unsung superpower of the platform — the experience is structured around trying, not committing.

Cross-border shipping. ASOS ships to South Africa for a flat fee under R200 and clears customs themselves on most orders under R3,000. Compare that to importing direct from UK brands (where you handle the SARS paperwork) and the value is obvious.

Brand discovery, controlled. The Edit collections are genuinely good. Whoever curates "Workwear Reimagined" and "Y2K Revival" is two trend cycles ahead of mainstream Instagram fashion.

Where ASOS still disappoints

  • ASOS DESIGN house brand quality is inconsistent. Stick to third-party where you can. The blazers and structured pieces are fine; the t-shirts and jeans are hit-and-miss.
  • Sizing varies wildly by brand. Don't assume your size in ASOS DESIGN is your size in Weekday or COS. Use the model height/measurements at the top of each product page — they're more accurate than the size charts.
  • Sale cycle is predictable. If you can wait 6 weeks, anything full-price now will be 30-50% off in their next storewide event. The Boxing Day, Black Friday, and Mid-Season sales are the only three worth setting calendar alerts for.

The play I run on ASOS

Build a saved list across 2-3 visits, never check out the first time. Wait for a 20%+ code (they appear monthly via the app, and Premier members get them first). Stack with the Premier free returns and you're effectively trying clothes on at home with no risk. The brand mix means I get a Carhartt jacket, a pair of Vans, and a COS knit in one parcel — three different stores' worth of shopping in one delivery.

The smartest thing ASOS ever did was making it feel low-stakes to order three sizes. Half my wardrobe came from "send-back-two" experiments that would have been "drive-to-three-stores" Saturdays in 2010.

If you only do one thing

Activate ASOS Premier on the very first order. Annual fee pays for itself in two parcels. From then on, treat the platform like a fitting room — order liberally, return ruthlessly, keep what fits.

Rakuten Global: the import shopping hack that's still hiding in plain sight

Most non-Japanese shoppers have no idea Rakuten Ichiba — the world's third-largest e-commerce marketplace — opened a real English storefront with international shipping. The catalogue is staggering and the prices on certain categories make it the single best site on the planet.

Rakuten Group in 2026 owns ~25% of Japanese e-commerce, runs a global ad network, and sits on a 100-million-user loyalty program. None of that matters to you. What matters is that Rakuten Global Express — the English-language gateway — lets you buy from any of 50,000+ Japanese merchants and ships worldwide, in many cases including delivery duty pre-paid.

If you've never shopped Japan from outside, the appetite-shift is the first surprise: the country exports premium consumer goods at prices that look like a typo. A 60-year-old Hario glass coffee dripper is ¥1,800 (about R220). A pair of Onitsuka Tiger Mexico 66 trainers is ¥9,900 (R1,200) — the same shoe is R2,400 at South African retailers. The maths is so favourable on certain categories that the shipping cost is irrelevant.

The five categories where Rakuten wins decisively

  • Japanese stationery and kitchenware. Tojiro knives, Hario glass, Snow Peak titanium. Half the price of Western retailers, before currency arbitrage.
  • Beauty and skincare. Hada Labo, Shiseido, DHC Cleansing Oil, Curél. Authentic-direct-from-Japan stock at 40-60% off Western prices.
  • Audio and electronics with Japan-domestic-market editions. Sony, Audio-Technica, and Denon often release JDM-spec versions with different drivers and tunings.
  • Vintage and collector goods. Pokémon cards, vintage Casio, vintage Seiko — the Japanese secondary market is honest and well-graded in a way eBay's "near mint" tag isn't.
  • Outdoor and workwear. Snow Peak, Helinox, Visvim, Beams Plus. Often the same garment is half the price as the US re-imported version.

The shipping math

Rakuten Global Express consolidates orders from multiple merchants into one parcel. You get one shipping fee, regardless of how many shops you order from. For shoppers in Africa, this is a game-changer: you can fill a virtual basket from twelve different Japanese specialists and pay one international airmail fee.

Customs: most orders to South Africa under R500 clear without VAT. Over that, expect 15% VAT plus a 7% ad valorem on luxury categories. Rakuten itself calculates and shows the duty estimate at checkout — they've gotten this part right, finally.

Where it gets tricky

Sizing on Japanese clothing runs two sizes smaller than Western equivalents. A Japanese L is roughly a Western M. Always check the centimetre chest/shoulder measurements on the listing. Returns from outside Japan are nearly impossible — treat every order as final. Don't gamble on fit; buy what you know.

Some merchants don't ship internationally even via the global platform. Look for the "ships worldwide" tag on each listing, not just on the site banner.

I bought a Tojiro Pro DP F-829 santoku from Rakuten in 2022 for $48. The same knife at Williams-Sonoma is $145. I cook with it every day. Compounding the price of being lazy adds up fast.

The play I run on Rakuten

Build a wishlist over a month. Wait for the monthly "Super Sale" — points multiplier events that drop effective prices another 15-25%. Consolidate orders to maximise the shipping economy. Rotate categories by season: kitchenware in winter, outdoor in spring, audio gear in autumn.

Zalando: the European answer to ASOS, with one significant advantage

If ASOS is a mid-priced department store online, Zalando is the upscale European version. The brand mix skews 30% more premium, the editorial taste is sharper, and there's a single trick — Zalando Lounge — that genuinely changes what you can afford.

Zalando is the largest fashion retailer in continental Europe — €11 billion in revenue, 50 million active customers, 6,000+ brands. The catalogue is deeper than ASOS in two specific areas: European mid-luxury (Acne Studios, Maison Kitsuné, A.P.C., Marni) and German engineering casuals (Adidas Originals JDM-equivalent stock, Salomon trail runners in colourways the US never sees).

The Lounge — the part nobody tells you about

Zalando Lounge is a separate, members-only flash-sale arm. It runs scheduled drops (usually Tuesday and Friday mornings, Berlin time) where overstock from the main site appears at 50-70% off. Stock is limited, sizes vanish in 90 minutes. But if you treat it like a regular shopping habit — check the new drops twice a week — you're buying €120 sneakers for €38, with the same return policy as the main site.

Lounge membership is free. The site is intentionally under-marketed to keep stock moving to existing members. Sign up, set browser notifications, and check after coffee on drop days.

Premium brand pricing — surprise winners

Zalando undercuts brand-owned sites on the following more than 80% of the time we've checked: Adidas Originals, New Balance Made in UK, Birkenstock, On Running, Asics, Salomon. The discount is usually 10-20% standard, 30-50% during their seasonal events. Brand-owned sites use full-price as marketing; Zalando uses competitive pricing as a customer-acquisition strategy.

Where Zalando loses to ASOS

  • Shipping outside Europe. ASOS ships to South Africa as part of its core operation; Zalando ships mainly within the EU and to a small list of additional countries. International shipping from Zalando is more complex and slower.
  • House brand depth. ASOS DESIGN, for all its faults, has more SKUs than Zalando's house brand Zalando Essentials.
  • Returns logistics outside EU. The "free returns within 100 days" promise is real in the EU and a different beast for cross-border orders.

Where Zalando is unbeatable

  • European fashion week brands — A.P.C., Acne, Maison Kitsuné, Carhartt WIP main collection — at consistent stocking levels.
  • German precision sportswear at street pricing.
  • Adidas Spezial drops — Zalando consistently gets them.
I waited 18 months for a specific colourway of On Cloudmonsters. Brand-direct: €170. Zalando: €98 during the Mid-Season Sale. Same shoe, same warranty.

The play

Use the main site for finding what you want. Use the Lounge for buying it. Set a search alert and let the platform do the work — patience is the only premium here.

Sephora's Beauty Insider program is the most underestimated loyalty scheme in retail

Beauty pricing online is a mess of "exclusive" flash sales, gift-with-purchase confusion, and creator-pushed launches. Sephora cuts through all of it with one program that, used properly, gives you a 20% effective discount on every purchase, every year.

Sephora's Beauty Insider tiers are: Insider (free), VIB (spend $350+/year), Rouge (spend $1,000+/year). The membership tiers don't unlock discounts directly — they unlock the right to save during four "Beauty Insider Sale" windows per year, where members get 10-20% off depending on tier.

Here's the under-appreciated part: those sales include almost every prestige brand, including the ones that never go on sale anywhere else. Skin Inc., Charlotte Tilbury, Drunk Elephant, Augustinus Bader, Tom Ford Beauty — all 20% off, twice a year, if you're Rouge. The 20% on a $300 Augustinus Bader serum is the difference between buying it and admiring it on Instagram.

The four sale windows you set calendar alerts for

  • Spring Savings Event — April. Rouge gets first access (5 days), VIB next (3 days), Insider last (3 days).
  • Summer Bonus — May. Smaller, but useful for restocking sunscreen and travel-size for holiday season.
  • Holiday Savings — November, overlapping Black Friday. The deepest discount of the year.
  • Year-end — late December. Targets stock clearance; the discount is smaller but the inventory has interesting items.

Birthday gifts: the small thing that matters

Every member gets a free birthday gift, redeemable in the month of your birthday. The gift is announced each January; in 2026 it's a Drunk Elephant duo, a Sephora Collection lip kit, or a 100 Beauty Insider Points option. The 100 Points option is the move if you're stockpiling for a big redemption — those points are worth around $15-20 of full-size product redemption value.

The points game

Points-for-products is a deal whose value drops as the prize tier goes up. The 100-point tier ($10 ish equivalent) usually offers $25-40 worth of product. The 1,000-point tier ($100 equivalent) typically offers $80-150 worth. The 5,000-point tier (mostly Rouge-exclusive) offers experiences, masterclasses, and one-of-a-kind items — these are status symbols more than value, but if you'd buy them anyway, the points trade is fine.

Where Sephora disappoints

  • Shipping costs for non-Rouge customers without a code can be eye-watering. Rouge gets free standard shipping; everyone else needs to hit the free-shipping threshold each time.
  • "Limited edition" exhaustion. The site cycles too many limited-edition launches; this trains shoppers to FOMO-buy at full price. Resist.
  • Sephora Collection (house brand) is fine, not exceptional. Use it for tools (brushes, sponges), not formulas.

The play

Sign up for free Insider. Track your spending across the year — if you'll hit $350, hold the order until you can put it through during a Beauty Insider Sale, when it'll cost 10-15% less. If you're a brand-loyalist on a $200/year prestige skincare habit, the Beauty Insider Sale alone saves you $40 a year. The birthday gift adds another $15-20. The tier is free. The maths is obvious.

Home · Global 9 min read By the Got It Superstore editors

Wayfair's sale calendar is more important than its catalogue

Furniture pricing online is a fiction — every retailer marks up, then "discounts," then marks up again. Wayfair is one of the worst offenders. Which is why understanding their sale calendar is the single most valuable skill for anyone shopping the site.

Wayfair carries 22 million products across home, garden, and outdoor categories. The catalogue is bigger than IKEA's by an order of magnitude. The site's strength is selection; its weakness is consistency — list prices are almost meaningless, and what looked like a "30% off" sofa was 35% off two weeks earlier on the same SKU. The CamelCamelCamel-style price-tracking habit that works on Amazon does not work here, because Wayfair routinely runs the same SKU at three different list prices in the same quarter.

The five sale events that actually matter

  • Way Day — late April / early May. Wayfair's flagship event, 36-72 hours, the deepest sitewide discount of the year. 60% off select pieces is common, 80% off on outdoor furniture during the right hour.
  • Memorial Day weekend — late May. Outdoor furniture and patio gets 40-60%; indoor is moderate.
  • Fourth of July — early July. Outdoor clearance hits its peak.
  • Labor Day — early September. The "back to indoor" pivot — sofas, area rugs, dining tables.
  • Black Friday / Cyber Monday — late November. Mattresses and electronics see their deepest cuts.

Outside these five windows, treat list prices as the ceiling and assume you can do 15-25% better with a small wait.

The Wayfair Professional program

Wayfair Professional is free to join (despite the name) for anyone who can demonstrate any business use — small Etsy seller, interior designer, contractor, even regular landlord. The benefits: dedicated phone support, a wider catalogue, and a 5-15% trade discount on most categories. It's not advertised; you need to apply via the homepage footer link.

What Wayfair does well

  • Wide selection at the mid-range. $400-$1,500 sofas in every imaginable style.
  • Outdoor furniture. The best online catalogue, especially for sectional patio sets and pergolas.
  • Area rugs. The selection by size, fibre, and price tier is unmatched.
  • White-glove delivery. Available on most furniture pieces over $500.

What Wayfair does badly

  • Brand consistency. Many products are private-label, sold under multiple names. The same coffee table can appear under "Mercury Row," "Brayden Studio," and "Three Posts" with three different prices.
  • Returns on large items. Possible, but you pay shipping unless the item is defective. A $400 chair you don't like costs $80 to return.
  • Reviews. Wayfair aggregates reviews across slight variants of the same SKU. A "4.7-star" sofa can have 4.7 from the velvet version and 2.1 from the linen — same listing.

The play

If you're not in a hurry, wait. The next Wayfair sale is never more than 8 weeks away. If you find a piece you love, save it, screenshot the SKU number, then check it during Way Day or the next major event. The same SKU often shifts 20-40% in price during sale windows. Furniture is one of the few categories where patience genuinely pays.

I screenshot every Wayfair piece I like into one Notes file. When Way Day hits, I open the file, run each SKU through the search bar, and buy the ones that have dropped 30% or more. Last Way Day that filter caught four items. I saved $640 with 20 minutes of attention.

Shein: a guide for shoppers who want to use it without pretending the trade-offs aren't real

Shein has become the world's largest fashion retailer by selling clothes most adults wouldn't have considered in 2017. The trade-offs around labour, sustainability, and quality are real — and so are the genuinely useful categories where Shein outperforms its premium competitors.

Two things are simultaneously true about Shein: the platform has supply-chain practices that have been credibly criticised by labour-rights organisations, the EU, and the US Congress. And — distinct from that — Shein produces certain categories of product at a price-quality ratio nothing else on Earth can match. Many shoppers want to navigate both facts honestly. So let's.

Where Shein quality is genuinely fine

  • Knitwear under R200 / $12. Basic crew-neck jumpers, ribbed cardigans, knit dresses. The yarn quality is consistent and the construction holds up to 30+ washes.
  • Activewear basics. Leggings, sports bras, gym tanks. Half the price of Lululemon's nearest equivalents and the performance gap is smaller than the price gap suggests.
  • Trend-cycle pieces. If a style will be embarrassing in 14 months — and most micro-trends are — paying $80 for it at a department store feels worse than paying $14 at Shein. This is the honest case for buying fast fashion.
  • Children's clothes sized 0-7. They'll grow out of these in months anyway.

Where Shein quality falls apart

  • Tailored or structured pieces. Blazers, suits, dress shirts. Linings tear, stitching unravels under any tension. Don't buy these.
  • Anything denim. The denim is inconsistent and the cuts are stretched-out versions of the photos. Skip.
  • Shoes. A category-wide quality problem. Soles separate, leather (always synthetic, even when labelled) cracks within a season.
  • Anything advertised as "leather." It's PU plastic that fakes leather poorly.

The reality on labour and supply chain

Multiple investigations between 2022 and 2025 — by Public Eye, the BBC, the US Congressional Select Committee, and the EU — documented overlong working hours, sub-living wages, and inadequate facility safety in parts of Shein's supplier network. Shein has rolled out a "Supplier Community Empowerment Programme" in response, but independent auditors have not yet validated that fixes are universal. We're not in a position to tell you whether this should change your decision. We are in a position to tell you that the "Forever 21 cost the same" defence isn't really true — Shein's prices are 30-60% below the previous fast-fashion floor, which has price-pressure implications upstream that anyone shopping the platform should at least be aware of.

The five rules we follow

  • Never buy what you'd buy at a department store anyway — pay for the better version of investment pieces, save the Shein budget for trend churn.
  • Buy sized up; the cut is consistently snug.
  • Skip the size-recommender; it's optimistic. Read 5 user reviews mentioning your body type.
  • Returns are possible but slow and costly (you pay return shipping outside the US). Treat purchases as final-ish.
  • Use the app, not the site — the app has the genuinely useful AR features for fit, and the prices in the app are 5-10% lower than the web.

The play

If you're going to shop fast fashion at all, do it consciously. Shein is the most efficient way to buy "throwaway trend" pieces while not pretending. Use it sparingly, never for staples, never for shoes, never for anything you intend to keep more than 12 months. Save your budget for the items you'll keep ten years and buy those at ASOS, Zalando, or directly from brands that pay their factories properly.

Apple's refurbished store is the cheapest official way to buy Apple — and almost nobody uses it

Apple does not discount new products. Period. But Apple does sell certified-refurbished hardware at 15-25% off retail, with the same one-year warranty as new, the same packaging, the same shipping. The catch: stock is limited, and the page is buried two layers deep on the website.

The Apple Certified Refurbished store stocks: MacBook Pro and Air (1-year-old and 2-year-old models), iPad Pro and Air, Apple TV, AirPods Max, and HomePod. Iconic absences: iPhones (Apple no longer sells refurbished iPhones in most regions — they go through carrier networks instead) and Apple Watch (similarly restricted in most regions).

What "Certified Refurbished" means: each unit is functionally tested, has a new battery and outer shell if needed, gets a new white box, and ships with the same accessories as new. Cosmetically you cannot tell the difference. The discount is roughly 15% for newest-generation refurbs, scaling to 25-30% for previous-generation models.

The seven-day window

Apple's refurbished stock turns over quickly. Popular configurations (16GB / 1TB MacBook Pro) sell out within 24-48 hours of appearing. The site refreshes inventory once a week on Tuesdays, US Eastern morning, but smaller drops happen randomly. If you want a specific configuration, build a saved search on the Refurb tracking site of your choice (there are a half-dozen free options) and accept that you'll buy what's there when it's there.

Back to School / Education Pricing

The Apple Education Store offers ~10% off Mac and iPad, plus a free pair of AirPods or accessory bundle during the annual Back to School promotion (July-September in most regions). Eligibility: any student or any university faculty or staff. Apple verifies your status via UNiDAYS or Authoritative Education Software service; the verification is light-touch and the discount applies for the entire calendar year of your enrolment.

Configuration arbitrage

Apple's BTO (build-to-order) markup on RAM and storage upgrades is brutal. A 512GB → 1TB SSD upgrade at order is $200; the equivalent external Thunderbolt SSD with the same speed is $90. For desktop users, this is a clear win — buy the smaller base storage and add external. For laptop users where portability matters, the calculus is different.

Trade-in

Apple Trade-In offers are often 20-30% below what you'd get on Swappa, Back Market, or eBay for the same device. The trade-off is convenience: Apple's trade-in is instant credit, in-store or at order time. Versus the hassle of listing, shipping, and dealing with buyer disputes elsewhere. If the difference is $80 and your time is worth more than that, Apple Trade-In wins.

Where Apple loses

  • Accessories. First-party cables, adapters, and chargers carry a 200-400% markup vs. Anker, UGREEN, or Belkin equivalents. Buy non-Apple for accessories.
  • AppleCare+. Worth it for the iPhone, marginal for the MacBook (the build is durable enough that mechanical failure within 3 years is rare), almost never worth it for the iPad.
  • iCloud storage. The 2TB iCloud+ plan is overpriced vs. Google One. If you don't need the Apple-specific features, Google One offers more for less.

The play

Plan Apple purchases 8-14 weeks in advance, monitor the refurb store via a tracker, and accept "the previous-generation Pro model" instead of "the new model that just launched." For desktops, buy the base RAM/storage and add external. For laptops, buy as much storage as you'll need in 5 years — it's the one thing you can't upgrade later.

Etsy in 2026: still magic, if you know how to filter out the dropshippers

For about a decade Etsy was the cleanest handmade-and-vintage marketplace on the internet. Then dropshippers found it. Then AliExpress resellers found it. The platform's identity is now an argument — but used carefully, it remains the best place to commission a piece of work that didn't exist before you asked for it.

Etsy's seller base sits around 9 million as of late 2025. The platform's enforcement of the original "handmade or vintage" rule has slipped — particularly since the 2022 seller strike — and a meaningful percentage of listings are now either dropshipped from AliExpress or "designed" by the seller and produced via print-on-demand services. The good sellers are still there; you just need a sharper filter.

The four-filter test for every seller

  • Shop "About" page. Real sellers show their workspace, hands, tools. AliExpress dropshippers show stock images of a curated lifestyle.
  • Process photos in listings. Real makers post in-progress shots. Dropshippers don't.
  • Production location. Reachable via the listing's right-side info panel. If a "Handmade in Brooklyn" listing also ships in 2 days from a non-US warehouse, something is off.
  • Number of identical recent reviews. Real handmade sellers receive review variation (size, customisation, fit). Dropshippers get template-style 5-star reviews with the same phrasing.

The Etsy categories worth using

  • Personalised ceramics, wood, leather. Etsy's actual sweet spot — commissioned work at fair prices.
  • Wedding stationery. Independent designers offer the best invitation suites at a quarter of the corporate-stationery-company prices.
  • Vintage 20th century goods. Mid-century furniture, vintage clothing pre-2000, vinyl, vintage watches — still well-curated by genuine collectors.
  • Knitting and yarn supplies. Indie dyers selling small-batch yarn directly.
  • Sewing patterns. Indie pattern designers are why the modern sewing renaissance exists.

The categories where Etsy loses

  • Phone cases, jewellery under $20, "engraved" generic items. Mostly print-on-demand. Buy at AliExpress at half the price if you want the same product.
  • Pet bandanas / kids' graphic tees. Sublimation printing on imported blanks. The "design" is the only thing the seller did.
  • Digital downloads. Heavily plagiarised. Many "printable wall art" listings use copyrighted images and AI-generated content without attribution.

The Star Seller signal

"Star Seller" is Etsy's badge for sellers meeting three criteria over the prior three months: response rate, on-time shipping, and 5-star reviews. The badge is meaningful — it filters out the bottom 80% of sellers — but it doesn't catch dropshippers, who often hit Star Seller criteria mechanically. Use it as a floor, not as a proof of authenticity.

The play

Use Etsy for commissions and personalisation. Use the search filter "Star Seller" + sort "Top customer reviews." Cross-check the seller's process photos. Buy with the assumption that you're supporting a real maker — and if you're not, leave and look elsewhere. The platform's value depends on enough buyers acting like real-maker advocates.

I commissioned a leather card holder from a Texas maker in 2021 for $48. Five years later it has visible wear in exactly the places I touch it daily. That's not damage; that's the entire point.

Auctions & resale · Global 8 min read By the Got It Superstore editors

eBay is still the most underrated marketplace for one specific shopper: the patient one

Everyone has heard of eBay. Most people remember it from 2008. The platform of 2026 is unrecognisable in two specific ways — and ignoring it costs the average careful shopper hundreds of dollars a year on the products eBay quietly does best.

eBay's 2026 identity is two distinct businesses inside one site. Half the listings are "Buy It Now" — essentially Amazon-style fixed-price retail. The other half are auctions, with the same end-of-bid theatre eBay was built on. Most casual buyers conflate them and miss the strategic difference: Buy It Now is for products you need now; auctions are for products you want eventually.

The categories eBay leads

  • Refurbished electronics. Apple Certified Refurbished, Samsung Certified Refurbished, and "Manufacturer Refurbished" listings all run through eBay's authenticated channels with 1-year warranties. Prices 20-35% below retail with the same protection.
  • Pre-owned watches under $5,000. eBay's Authenticity Guarantee program inspects every watch over $2,000 through an independent appraiser. The fraud risk that defined the platform in 2010 is operationally solved.
  • Vintage trading cards and collectibles. The market-clearing price for any Pokémon card, Magic: The Gathering rarity, or sports card is the eBay sold-listings price. This is the real index.
  • Discontinued parts. Lens hoods for film cameras, replacement keys for vintage IBM keyboards, OEM phone parts. eBay is sometimes the only source.
  • Wholesale-bulk overruns. Sealed cases of stationery, deadstock fragrances, factory-second tools. Search "lot of" + the product category to find these.

The auction strategy that works

The folklore says "snipe in the final 10 seconds." That's correct, but the why matters more than the when: bidding earlier signals interest and recruits competing bidders. Bidding once, late, at your true maximum, denies that signal. Use a sniping service or your platform's auto-bid feature and walk away. The single biggest mistake new eBay bidders make is bidding multiple times — every additional bid lifts the final price by an average of 4-8%.

How "Best Offer" actually works

"Best Offer" listings let you submit a price below the asking. Most sellers respond within 24 hours; many accept around 70-85% of asking. The optimal first offer is 68% of asking — high enough that the seller engages, low enough to leave negotiating room. The negotiation pattern is well-understood; behave like a serious buyer and the discount is yours.

What eBay does badly

  • Apparel. Returns are seller-by-seller and unreliable. Don't bother.
  • Generic home goods. Dropshippers everywhere. AliExpress is faster and cheaper for the same product.
  • "Brand new with tags" listings under suspicious sellers. The category for counterfeit. If you wouldn't trust the offer on the street, don't trust it here.

The play

Use eBay for refurbished electronics, vintage parts, collectible authentication, and patient auction wins on items you've been wanting for months anyway. Sort by "Sold Items" before buying — it shows you the actual market price for the SKU over the last 90 days, regardless of what the current listing claims.

Global wholesale 9 min read By the Got It Superstore editors

AliExpress is the upstream of half the internet — and exactly the same products at exactly less money

If you've bought a phone case, a USB-C cable, a kitchen gadget, or anything described as "innovative" on Instagram, there's a 70% chance you've already bought from AliExpress without knowing it. Once you understand that, the savings get serious.

AliExpress is the Alibaba Group's consumer-facing channel — a platform connecting Chinese factories and traders directly with end consumers worldwide. Most of what you see in "innovative product" Instagram ads is sourced from here at 10-30% of the price the ad sells it at. The Stanley-cup-knockoff that's "designed in California"? Often the same factory, same mould, same product, with a different sticker.

What "buy direct" actually buys you

The classic example: a "wireless desk fan" that's $35 on Amazon, $48 in a curated home-goods store, and $5.20 on AliExpress with 25-day shipping. The fan is identical. The premium you're paying elsewhere is for two things: faster shipping, and the curation-and-warranty layer the reseller provides. Decide what those things are worth to you, and shop accordingly.

The categories where AliExpress is dominant

  • Phone cases and accessories. Identical to what's sold elsewhere at 5-15% of the price.
  • USB-C / Thunderbolt / charging cables. The good factories are listed; cross-reference USB-IF certified options.
  • LED lighting. Aputure, Godox, Neewer — most are made by the same handful of factories. Buying direct skips the brand markup entirely.
  • Tools. Wera-clone screwdrivers, JBL-spec speakers, magnetic levels, multimeters. The clones are 80% of the performance at 25% of the price.
  • Cosplay, costume, and craft supplies. No-one else competes on these.
  • Watch straps. $4 ZULUDIVER-spec NATO straps in 50 colourways.

The Choice program

AliExpress Choice, launched in 2023, is the platform's version of Amazon Prime: pre-vetted sellers, 5-12 day shipping, full refund guarantees. Choice listings are the right starting point for anyone new to the platform. Look for the Choice badge (a yellow-and-red ribbon on the listing thumbnail) and treat non-Choice listings as a slightly higher-risk option that's still acceptable if the seller has 4.7+ rating and 500+ orders.

Customs and import duty for South African buyers

Orders under R500 generally clear without VAT. Between R500-R2,000, expect 15% VAT plus a small clearance fee. AliExpress now calculates and pre-collects most of this at checkout — far smoother than 2018. Larger orders, or anything classified as "luxury" (watches, fragrances, brand-name goods), may attract additional ad valorem duty; the platform's duty estimator is reasonably accurate.

The five rules

  • Only buy from sellers with 1,000+ orders on the listing.
  • Always read 5 of the most recent negative reviews — they're more informative than 50 positives.
  • Never buy electronics from no-name sellers; insist on a known factory brand at minimum.
  • Use Choice for first orders; expand to other sellers once you're confident.
  • Treat 25-30 day shipping as the floor for non-Choice orders. The 7-day promise is often optimistic.

The play

Build a wishlist over a month, time it around the 11.11 Singles Day sale or the 6.18 Mid-Year sale (the two biggest AliExpress sale events). Discounts in those windows are real — 30-50% off Choice listings, with stacking coupons. Buy in bulk for non-perishable categories (cables, batteries, craft supplies), single-buy for clothing where fit is a question mark.

Takealot for South Africans: the home-field advantage in a global mall

If you're shopping from South Africa, the maths on Takealot is fundamentally different than on every other store in this guide. Local stock, local delivery, local returns — and a market position that means competitors have to work harder to beat its everyday prices.

Takealot owns roughly 60% of the South African e-commerce market. The platform is Naspers-backed, locally warehoused in Gauteng, Cape Town, and Durban, with same-day delivery available in the major metro areas. For Saffer shoppers, the Takealot tradeoffs that matter aren't about it versus Amazon — Amazon doesn't seriously operate here yet — but about Takealot versus Loot, Yuppiechef, and the manufacturer-direct stores.

Where Takealot is unbeatable

  • Same-day and next-day delivery. Pickup points and Takealot Day delivery have a logistics moat that no competitor has matched. Order before 12pm in Gauteng metros, get next-day. The Cape Town and Durban networks are nearly as good.
  • The Daily Deals. Refreshed at 7am each morning. Real discounts on real stock — usually electronics, home, and small appliances. Subscribe to the daily email; the deals are skim-able in 90 seconds and the best ones sell out by lunch.
  • Takealot Plus. R99 per month or R649 per year. Free standard delivery on all orders, plus Plus-exclusive deals. If you order more than five times a year, the maths obviously favours subscribing.
  • Returns. 30-day returns via the same delivery network. No "in-store only" friction. Refunds typically process within 5 business days.

Where Takealot disappoints

  • The marketplace seller listings. Like Amazon, Takealot has third-party sellers fulfilling alongside Takealot Retail. The marketplace sellers' quality is highly variable; read recent reviews before buying anything fulfilled by a marketplace seller you don't recognise.
  • Pricing on specialty categories. Yuppiechef beats Takealot on premium kitchenware. Loot beats Takealot on books. Cape Union Mart and Cycle Lab beat Takealot on outdoor and cycling. For anything specialist, check the category leader's site before defaulting to Takealot.
  • Authentic-brand drift. Some categories — particularly cosmetics, supplements, and certified watches — have intermittent counterfeit issues on marketplace listings. Stick to "Sold & Shipped by Takealot" for these.

The Black Friday rhythm

Takealot's Blue Dot Sale (Black Friday week) is the deepest local discount cycle of the year. The pattern is consistent: electronics see 25-45% cuts, small appliances 30-50%, furniture 20-40%. Plus subscribers get early access to the deepest cuts. If you can plan a major purchase around late November, you'll save 30%+ vs. the same SKU in March.

The Takealot vs. import calculation

For most consumer electronics under R5,000, Takealot beats importing from AliExpress or eBay once you factor in shipping, customs, and the risk of fakes. For premium electronics over R5,000, the calculation reverses — direct import (with extended warranty layered through a local repair shop) usually wins by 15-25%.

Takealot's logistics network is the unsung South African e-commerce success story. We forget how quickly it became normal to order something Friday evening and have it Monday morning. That's the floor, not the ceiling.

The play

Default to Takealot for anything you need quickly. Wishlist things you don't need urgently and check them during the next Daily Deal cycle or the Blue Dot Sale. Subscribe to Plus if your annual order count exceeds five. Use the app, not the site — the app's deal alerts are 12-24 hours faster than the email.

IKEA online: where the catalogue is bigger than the showroom and the trick is knowing which pieces age well

Some IKEA pieces are iconic and last decades. Others fall apart within a year. The catalogue makes no distinction between the two. After 80 years and 12 IKEA homes' worth of accumulated furniture, here's the working shopper's guide to which IKEA pieces are investments and which are temporary.

IKEA's design philosophy — affordable, flat-packed, designed in Sweden, manufactured globally — produces a catalogue with extraordinary range. The Pax wardrobe and the Stockholm leather sofa share a brand name but almost nothing else. The first will outlast three apartments; the second will lose its shape in two years. The discipline IKEA shoppers need is knowing which is which.

The pieces that outlast everything

  • Pax wardrobes. Modular, deeply customisable, lasts 15+ years with no visible degradation. The hinges and drawer slides are German-made and silent.
  • Kallax cube shelving. The four-by-four version has been in our office for nine years across three moves. Still square, still load-bearing.
  • Poäng armchair. Three generations in production. The bentwood frame is genuinely structural; only the cushion ages.
  • Billy bookcase. Yes, the laminate is laminate. But over 1.1 billion units sold over 45 years — the design is correct.
  • Malm bed frame. Stable, simple, durable. The under-bed drawers version is the unsung version.
  • Linnmon / Adils desk. The legs-and-top kit is the cheapest competent home-office desk in retail.

The pieces that don't last

  • Particle-board side tables. Lack and Lack-derivatives — fine for college, not for keeping. They warp under any humidity, swell at the corners, and water rings stain immediately.
  • Mid-range fabric sofas. Friheten, Vimle's lower configurations. The covers go shabby fast; the frames are fine but the upholstery isn't.
  • Pendant lights with painted finish. The paint chips off the lamp housing within a year.
  • Most kitchen tools. The cookware (365+) is decent; the knives, peelers, and graters are not.
  • Outdoor furniture in particle board / acacia. Acacia is rated outdoor but oxidises grey within a season; warranty is conditional on oiling every quarter.

IKEA delivery in South Africa

IKEA does not operate stores in South Africa as of 2026 (the long-rumoured Cape Town store has been delayed indefinitely). Online ordering via the global site can be done, but shipping is expensive and complicated for flat-pack items. Workaround: most South African design retailers carry IKEA-equivalent products from local manufacturers — @home, Eight Days A Week, House & Home, and Coricraft offer comparable modular wardrobes and shelving at competitive prices, with local delivery and assembly. The IKEA brand premium isn't worth what it costs to ship across the Atlantic.

The assembly reality

The famous Allen-key meme is mostly fair. Skill required: low. Time required: usually 2-3x the box estimate. The biggest gains in IKEA assembly come from one thing: laying out every component on the floor first and checking against the parts list before starting. Skipping that step is responsible for 90% of "missing screw" disasters.

The play

Buy the lasting pieces (Pax, Kallax, Poäng, Billy, Malm, Linnmon) without hesitation; they outlive the price and become design staples. Avoid the disposable categories (Lack tables, painted lighting, particle-board outdoor) regardless of price. For non-IKEA markets, find your local equivalent — @home in South Africa, Habitat in the UK, Muji in Asia — and apply the same "buy the right pieces" filter.

The Costco membership maths: when the $65 annual fee is the best deal in retail

Most "membership shopping" calculations are pretty arithmetic. The Costco one is unusually interesting because the membership has an asymmetric return — it's either pure profit or wasted money, with very little middle ground. Here's the honest version.

Costco's Gold Star membership is $65/year in the US ($130 for Executive, which adds 2% cashback on most purchases). In countries where Costco operates (US, Canada, UK, Japan, Korea, Mexico, Spain, Australia, and more), the equivalent local pricing applies. To break even on the membership, you need roughly $300-400 of annual savings vs. the same items at a regular retailer. For households of 2+, that bar is trivial. For singles or two-people-living-alone, the bar takes some thought.

The five categories where Costco wins decisively

  • Kirkland Signature private-label. Quality is genuinely competitive with name brands, prices 20-30% lower. Specific standout SKUs: Kirkland nuts, olive oil, batteries, vitamins, frozen seafood, paper goods.
  • Eyewear and hearing aids. Costco Optical's prices on prescription glasses are 30-50% below independent optometrists. Hearing aids — historically a $4,000-$6,000 purchase — are $1,400-$2,000 at Costco with the same brands.
  • Tyres. Costco Tire Center installation includes free rotation, balance, flat repair, nitrogen fills, and a tread-life warranty for the life of the tyre. The all-in cost beats Discount Tire and Walmart by 5-15%.
  • Gasoline (where applicable). Costco gas is consistently 10-30¢ per gallon below local averages. For a 20,000-mile-per-year driver, the gas savings alone pay for the membership 2-3 times over.
  • Bulk meat and seafood for households of 4+. $7-9/lb for prime grade beef vs. $11-14 at supermarkets. The freezer commitment is real but the savings are obvious.

The five categories where Costco loses

  • Fresh produce in small quantities. The 5-lb bag of strawberries goes off if you can't eat 5 lbs of strawberries.
  • Specialty / niche products. Costco doesn't carry the long tail. Need a specific brand of hot sauce? Probably not there.
  • Books and games. Sometimes priced well; sometimes not. Amazon usually still wins on book pricing.
  • Cleaning supplies for tiny households. 16 boxes of dishwasher tablets is a 4-year supply for a single person. Pass.
  • Anything you're not certain you'll use up. "Bulk discount" only matters if you use the bulk.

The Executive math

The Executive Membership ($65 upgrade from Gold Star, total $130) earns 2% back on Costco purchases, capped at $1,000 of cashback per year. The break-even on the upgrade is $3,250 of annual Costco spend. For households spending more than that, Executive is a clear win — you get the $65 upgrade back plus another 2% net positive. For households spending under that, stick with Gold Star.

The non-shopping benefits worth knowing

  • Costco Travel. Cruise, hotel, and car rental rates that are competitive but rarely the absolute lowest. Useful for bundled vacation packages.
  • Costco Auto Program. Pre-negotiated dealer pricing for new and used cars. Average reported savings $1,000-$1,500 vs. walking in cold to a dealership.
  • Costco Pharmacy. Prescription drug prices are routinely 30-50% below competitors. Membership not required to use the pharmacy in most US states.

The play

If you're a household of two adults who eat meat, drive a car, wear glasses, or use coffee — sign up for the Gold Star. Track your savings for the first six months; if you've banked more than $130 of difference vs. retail prices on the same items, upgrade to Executive at any time and Costco will pro-rate. If you're a single person living in a city, the maths is harder; lean on the gas, tyres, and Optical wins to make the membership work, and skip the bulk frozen seafood.

The Costco membership is the most asymmetric trade in personal finance: $65 of risk to save thousands. For most middle-class households, that's not a decision — that's just doing the obvious thing.
