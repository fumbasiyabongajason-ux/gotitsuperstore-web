How to shop ASOS from South Africa

ASOS does deliver to South Africa — but the sticker price isn't the whole story. Here's how to order, what duties to expect since 2024, and how to avoid a surprise bill at the door.

ASOS is one of the most-searched international fashion stores in South Africa, and the good news is simple: yes, ASOS ships to South Africa. You order in the normal way, pay in your currency, and a courier handles the final delivery to your door. The catch is the landed cost — since SARS changed the import rules in 2024, clothing is taxed more heavily than it used to be, so the price you see is not the price you pay.

Does ASOS deliver to South Africa?

Yes. ASOS offers international delivery to South Africa, with standard and (often) express options at checkout. Delivery typically takes around one to two weeks for standard shipping, and ASOS usually offers free standard delivery once your order passes a spend threshold — below that, a flat delivery fee applies. Always confirm the exact fee, threshold and timeline at checkout, because ASOS adjusts these by region and season.

The part most people miss: import duties

Until 31 August 2024, small parcels under R500 slipped in under an old SARS concession. That concession is gone. Since 1 September 2024, import VAT of 15% applies on top of customs duty1, and clothing is one of the most heavily taxed categories — duty can reach roughly 45%, plus VAT.2 The courier normally collects this before final delivery, which is why an ASOS haul can feel far pricier than the basket total.

⚠️ Budget like a local pro: a long-standing rule of thumb among SA ASOS shoppers is to roughly double the price of a clothing item to estimate the true landed cost once shipping, duty and VAT are added. If it still feels worth it at that number, buy it.

How to keep the cost down

• Shop the sale. ASOS discounts deeply and often; a sale item that lands at double its price can still beat local retail.
• Consolidate one bigger order rather than several small ones, so you pay shipping and clearance once.
• Hit the free-delivery threshold if you're close to it — it's often cheaper than paying the flat fee twice.
• Declare the real value. Under-declaring to dodge duty risks fines and delays; it isn't worth it.
• Compare locally first for basics — for heavily-taxed staples, a South African store can win once duty and VAT are added.

Frequently asked questions

Does ASOS ship to South Africa?

Yes. ASOS delivers to South Africa with standard and often express options at checkout. Standard delivery usually takes around one to two weeks, and free delivery is typically offered once your order passes a spend threshold.

Do I pay customs duty on ASOS orders to South Africa?

Usually yes. Since SARS removed the small-parcel concession in 2024, imported clothing attracts customs duty (which can be around 45%) plus 15% VAT, normally collected by the courier before delivery. Budget for it before you buy.

How long does ASOS take to deliver to South Africa?

Standard international delivery to South Africa generally takes about one to two weeks, depending on the option chosen and customs clearance. Express options are sometimes available for a higher fee.

Is it cheaper to buy ASOS or shop locally in South Africa?

For unique pieces and deep sale items, ASOS can still win even after duty and VAT. For everyday basics, a local South African retailer is often cheaper once import taxes are added — so compare the landed cost first.
