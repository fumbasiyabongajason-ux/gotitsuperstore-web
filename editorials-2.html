Wing 2 · Editorial Vol. 2

Thirty stores, thirty honest snapshots.

Each store in Wing 2 gets two paragraphs — what they're best at, and what to buy first. Quicker than the long-form Vol. 1, useful for finding your way through the global floor.

Updated May 2026 · ~7,500 words across 30 pieces · All ship globally
🇨🇳
Marketplace · China

JD.com — China's most reliable marketplace

🌐 Ships to 200+ countriesElectronics

JD.com is the marketplace Chinese tech-enthusiasts actually shop on. Stricter authenticity controls than AliExpress, owns its own logistics network, fulfilment within 24 hours inside China and 7-15 days to most countries. The English JD Global site handles customs paperwork for you.

Buy first: branded electronics — Xiaomi, Huawei, Lenovo, OnePlus, DJI. Authentic stock at JD-direct prices, 20-40% under Western retail. Skip the third-party seller listings; stick with "Sold by JD".

Shop JD.com →
🇹🇷
Marketplace · Turkey

Trendyol — Turkish fashion at factory prices

🌐 Ships to 195 countriesFashion

Turkey is one of the world's largest garment manufacturers, and Trendyol is the marketplace where most of that production sells directly. Strong on contemporary fashion, modest wear, and traditional Turkish textiles. International shipping is faster than you'd expect — typically 5-10 days.

Buy first: well-tailored basics (blazers, trousers, dresses) at 40-60% under Western prices, and authentic Turkish towels & bathrobes (the world's best, full stop). Use the Trendyol Global site, not the Turkish one.

Shop Trendyol →
🌟
Luxury · Global

Farfetch — luxury aggregated from 1,400 boutiques

🌐 Ships globallyLuxury fashion

Farfetch doesn't sell its own stock — it aggregates inventory from 1,400 independent luxury boutiques worldwide. Gucci, Prada, Saint Laurent, The Row, Balenciaga, Loewe — all routed through one checkout. The catch is shipping comes from the specific boutique you're buying from.

Buy first: the two seasonal Farfetch Sale events (late June, late December) where boutiques clear stock at 40-60% off. Pre-order styles also live here before they hit brand-direct sites — useful for popular drops.

Shop Farfetch →
⚫
Designer · Canada

SSENSE — designer marketplace with editorial taste

🌐 Ships globallyDesigner fashion

Canadian-based, Montreal-cool. SSENSE buys directly from 500+ designers — Acne Studios, Maison Margiela, Rick Owens, Lemaire, Comme des Garçons. Editorial photography is some of the best in the industry. Returns are easy. Cross-border duty is calculated at checkout, no surprises.

Buy first: end-of-season markdowns — SSENSE's sale page is regularly 50-70% off, with sizes that don't sell out as fast as on Farfetch. The men's tailoring section is unusually deep.

Shop SSENSE →
🧢
Streetwear · UK

END. — streetwear, sneakers, and serious raffles

🌐 Ships globallyStreetwear / sneakers

END. (Newcastle, UK) is the source for streetwear, technical apparel, and sneakers that sell out everywhere else. Stüssy, Carhartt WIP, Stone Island, Acronym, Patagonia. The site also runs sneaker raffles for limited drops — Yeezys, collab Jordans, niche Adidas releases.

Buy first: if you're hunting a sneaker that's sold out elsewhere, check END.'s raffle calendar. For apparel, their final-sale markdowns hit 60% off twice a year and the catalogue stays available longer than most competitors.

Shop END. →
✨
Trendy fashion · US

Revolve — what every fashion creator quietly buys

🌐 Ships globallyContemporary fashion

Revolve carries 500+ contemporary labels — Reformation, House of Harlow, Lovers + Friends, Lavender Brown, Norma Kamali. The site dominates the influencer-fashion market because it's where the trend pieces land first. Returns are free in the US; international returns cost $9.

Buy first: the Revolve Clearance section runs deeper than most competitors — items are 60-80% off and there's no extra timer pressure. International shipping is calculated upfront with duties pre-paid, so no customs surprise.

Shop Revolve →
🟥
Essentials · Japan

Uniqlo — Japanese basics that outlast trends

🌐 50+ country sitesBasics

Uniqlo is the wardrobe-foundation brand of the 2020s. HEATTECH thermals, AIRism cooling layers, Ultra Light Down jackets, the U range with Lemaire, the +J range with Jil Sander. Quality at scale that competitors haven't matched. Country-specific sites give honest pricing.

Buy first: the Heattech crew-neck (for winter) and AIRism mesh tee (for summer) are the best base layers at their price point anywhere. The +J and U collaboration drops are short-window — buy the day they launch or wait for the next round.

Shop Uniqlo →
🎩
Men's luxury · UK

Mr Porter — Net-a-Porter's men's-only sibling

🌐 Ships globallyMen's luxury

Same parent company as Net-a-Porter, same editorial polish, men only. 600+ menswear and lifestyle brands. Mr Porter Style Council column is a genuine reference for how to dress at the budget you actually have. The two yearly End-of-Season Sales mirror NAP's — June and December.

Buy first: tailoring (Brunello Cucinelli, The Row, Belstaff). Mr Porter's tailoring discounts during sale events run deeper than brand-direct. Shoes (Edward Green, Crockett & Jones) at 40% off twice a year.

Shop Mr Porter →
💍
Luxury · UK

MatchesFashion — designer with rare-piece edge

🌐 Ships globallyDesigner fashion

Matches buys deeper into specific labels than competitors — Cecilie Bahnsen, Toteme, Sandy Liang, Wales Bonner, Khaite, Jacquemus, Bode. The catalogue feels like an editor's pick. Marketing emails are genuinely useful. Their exclusive capsule collaborations are sought-after.

Buy first: the labels Matches stocks deeper than anyone else (especially Cecilie Bahnsen and Toteme). End-of-Season sales sometimes drop these brands to 70% off — uncommon elsewhere.

Shop MatchesFashion →
👚
Fast fashion · Sweden

H&M — the affordable wardrobe staple

🌐 75+ country sitesFast fashion

H&M is the original global fast-fashion player. 4,000 stores across 75 countries, online ships to most. Quality is variable — basics are reliable, statement pieces less so. The Conscious / Sustainable line uses recycled materials. H&M Members (free) unlock 10% off and free shipping over £20.

Buy first: structured pieces (blazers, trousers, knitwear) in the H&M Studio collection. Skip the very-trend pieces. The H&M x Designer collaborations (announced annually) are genuinely worth waking up for.

Shop H&M →
🖤
Fast fashion · Spain

Zara — Inditex's flagship moves fashion in days

🌐 96 country sitesContemporary fashion

Zara's supply chain pushes new designs from sketch to store in 2-3 weeks. Two major drops weekly. Trends spotted on runways appear in Zara before they appear on competitors. Better tailoring than its price suggests. The Zara HOME and SRPLS lines deserve more attention than they get.

Buy first: Zara's tailoring (blazers, trouser suits) is the secret weapon — better cut and finishing than the price implies. The Zara HOME catalogue has some of the best mid-priced ceramic and textile pieces in retail.

Shop Zara →
⚪
Premium minimalist · H&M Group

COS — quiet luxury at a quietly-fair price

🌐 50+ countriesPremium minimalist

COS is the H&M Group brand for shoppers who want The Row aesthetic without The Row prices. Architectural cuts, considered fabrics, restrained colour palettes. The closest mainstream alternative to Lemaire or Toteme. Twice-yearly sale events drop pieces 40-60% off.

Buy first: structured outerwear (the wool coats and double-breasted blazers especially). COS knitwear in seasonal colours is also a strong value — better hand than most premium-tier alternatives at half the price.

Shop COS →
🍃
Fashion · Spain

Mango — Spanish style with French restraint

🌐 90+ country sitesContemporary fashion

Mango sits between Zara and Massimo Dutti — more refined than Zara, more accessible than Massimo. Strong on tailored separates, dresses, and shoes. The Mango Selection range edges into premium territory with elevated fabrics and finishing.

Buy first: tailored trousers and the linen-suiting collection (the best linen-blend trousers at this price point in retail). Mango shoes are quietly competitive — leather quality close to Massimo Dutti at much lower prices.

Shop Mango →
🧥
Premium · Inditex

Massimo Dutti — Zara's grown-up sibling

🌐 90+ countriesPremium fashion

Same group as Zara, very different brief. Massimo Dutti is the office-wear brand for shoppers who want quality leather, real wool, and tailored cuts at premium-but-not-luxury prices. The men's range is unusually strong — particularly knitwear and casual blazers.

Buy first: leather goods. Massimo Dutti leather (jackets, bags, shoes) consistently outperforms similar-priced competitors. The men's "Limited Edition" line uses premium Italian fabrics at department-store-friendly prices.

Shop Massimo Dutti →
💗
Premium · H&M Group

& Other Stories — Stockholm-rooted, hand-finished

🌐 EU, US, Asia ship-outPremium fashion

& Other Stories sits alongside COS in the H&M premium portfolio, with a more romantic and feminine identity. Three design ateliers (Stockholm, Paris, LA) each produce distinct seasonal lines. Beauty range is unexpectedly good — their hand creams and perfumes punch above weight.

Buy first: dresses (their cut and proportion is consistently flattering across body types), and the in-house perfumes (£40 for genuinely sophisticated scents that compete with £150 prestige bottles).

Shop & Other Stories →
🛍️
Department store · UK

Selfridges — London's department-store benchmark

🌐 130+ countriesDepartment store

Selfridges' online catalogue mirrors its Oxford Street flagship — 3,000+ brands across fashion, beauty, food, home. Premium curation with a contemporary edge. Selfridges Exclusive collaborations (multiple per year) are genuinely rare-piece territory. International shipping is well-organised.

Buy first: the bi-annual Selfridges Sale (June and December). Discounts on luxury beauty are particularly deep — La Mer, Augustinus Bader, Sisley pricing can drop 25-30% which almost never happens at brand-direct.

Shop Selfridges →
🧴
Beauty · UK

Cult Beauty — niche brands curated

🌐 Ships globallyBeauty

Cult Beauty's editors handpick 300+ indie and prestige beauty brands. Drunk Elephant, Tatcha, ABH, Dr. Barbara Sturm, Augustinus Bader. New launches often appear here first. Their beauty boxes (twice a year) are some of the best-value sampler kits in the industry.

Buy first: the Goody Bag promotions — spend a threshold (usually £80-120) and get a curated bag worth £150-300. These run 4-6 times a year and clear faster than the brand-direct equivalents.

Shop Cult Beauty →
💅
Beauty · THG

Lookfantastic — beauty at near-wholesale

🌐 30+ country sitesBeauty

Owned by The Hut Group, Lookfantastic carries 800+ brands including the entire prestige lineup (La Roche-Posay, Vichy, Caudalie, Olaplex, ESPA). Pricing is consistently 5-15% below department-store equivalents, with regular 25% off codes available via the email signup.

Buy first: the monthly Lookfantastic Beauty Box (around £15/month, worth £60+) for trying high-end products at sample volumes. Useful for testing before committing to full-size purchases.

Shop Lookfantastic →
💋
Beauty marketplace · UK

Feelunique — Europe's largest online beauty store

🌐 120 countriesBeauty

Feelunique stocks 35,000 SKUs across 600 brands — including hard-to-find European pharmacy lines (Avène, Bioderma, Embryolisse, Klorane) that mainstream retailers don't carry. Owned by Sephora since 2021 but operates separately with deeper European catalogue.

Buy first: French pharmacy skincare. The classics — Bioderma Sensibio cleansing water, Avène Thermal Spring Water spray, Embryolisse Lait-Crème Concentré — are 30-50% cheaper than buying them outside France.

Shop Feelunique →
🌱
Ethical beauty · UK

The Body Shop — the ethics-first beauty originator

🌐 70+ countriesBeauty

Founded in 1976, The Body Shop pioneered the cruelty-free retail beauty movement decades before it became mainstream. Community Fair Trade ingredients sourced from 32 countries. Refill program reduces packaging at brick-and-mortar stores. Genuinely sustainable claims, audited.

Buy first: the Almond Milk & Honey body lotion, the Coconut shower cream, the Vitamin E moisturiser, and the Tea Tree clarifying line. These have been in continuous production for 30+ years for a reason. The Christmas gift sets are exceptional value.

Shop The Body Shop →
🌿
Health · US

iHerb — supplements and natural health, globally shipped

🌐 180+ countriesHealth & wellness

iHerb is the global supplement/vitamin/natural-health marketplace. 30,000 products from 1,400 brands — NOW Foods, Garden of Life, Jarrow Formulas, Doctor's Best, Bulletproof. Lab-tested. Pricing is significantly below local pharmacy markup in most countries. Shipping to South Africa runs $6-15 depending on weight.

Buy first: vitamins that are expensive at local pharmacies — Vitamin D3, magnesium glycinate, omega-3. iHerb's house brand California Gold Nutrition matches premium brand quality at roughly half the price.

Shop iHerb →
🥤
Sports nutrition · UK

MyProtein — the gym shopper's default

🌐 70+ country sitesSports nutrition

MyProtein owns the budget end of the sports-nutrition market through aggressive direct-to-consumer pricing. Impact Whey, Clear Whey, Vegan Blend, EAAs, creatine — all 30-50% cheaper than competitors. Quality is third-party tested. The store runs 30-40% off promotions weekly.

Buy first: Impact Whey Protein (chocolate smooth) is the cult product. The Clear Whey range (juice-style protein drinks) is the under-rated alternative for shoppers who hate milkshake textures. Buy during a sale event — never full price.

Shop MyProtein →
💪
Fitness apparel · UK

Gymshark — the influencer-fitness brand done right

🌐 130+ countriesFitness apparel

Started in a UK garage in 2012, Gymshark is now the dominant Gen-Z gym brand. Fits are flattering in a way the older sportswear brands don't quite match — particularly leggings and seamless tops. The Vital Seamless 2.0 collection is the platform product.

Buy first: Vital Seamless 2.0 leggings (in your usual size — they run accurate, unlike Lululemon). Wait for the Black Friday or Boxing Day sales — Gymshark discounts hard during those windows, typically 50-60% off.

Shop Gymshark →
🏃
Running · Switzerland

On Running — Swiss engineering, runner-loved

🌐 60+ countriesRunning shoes

Founded in Zurich in 2010, On Running's CloudTec sole technology — visible cushioning pods on the underside — became one of the most distinctive sneaker designs of the 2020s. The Cloudmonster (max cushion) and Cloudboom Echo 3 (race) are the standouts.

Buy first: the Cloudmonster for daily training, the Cloudultra 2 for trail. On rarely discounts brand-direct, but year-old colourways drop 25-35% during seasonal transitions (Mar/Apr and Aug/Sep). Direct shipping is fast and free in most markets over $100.

Shop On Running →
🚴
Cycling & sport · UK

Wiggle — cycling specialist, deeper than any generalist

🌐 70+ countriesCycling / triathlon

Wiggle's catalogue is the deepest cycling resource in online retail — Shimano, Specialized, Castelli, Rapha, Park Tool. Triathlon and outdoor running gear sit alongside. Free international shipping over £50 / $80 depending on destination. The clearance section is regularly 60% off.

Buy first: consumables (chains, brake pads, bar tape, tubes). Wiggle's bulk pricing on these consumables is unmatched. For bigger purchases, set price alerts — items often drop 25-35% during seasonal sales.

Shop Wiggle →
📷
Pro photo · audio · video · US

B&H Photo Video — the pro shooter's preferred address

🌐 225+ countriesPhoto / Pro AV

B&H is the largest professional photo / video / audio retailer in the US — family-owned since 1973, NYC superstore plus a 70,000-SKU online catalogue. Cameras, lenses, lighting, audio interfaces, computers, broadcast gear. Their used and refurbished section is the trust standard in the industry. Free standard US shipping; international shipping with full duty pre-paid at checkout.

Buy first: the Used Department for cameras and lenses 30-50% below retail with B&H's own warranty. The bi-annual Mega DealZone clearance events (March, September) discount entire kits at outlet pricing. For audio, the in-house "Hand Pick" reviews are genuinely useful.

Shop B&H Photo →
🧱
Toys · Denmark

LEGO — the toy that's actually a lifelong hobby

🌐 130+ countriesToys / collectibles

LEGO brand-direct stocks the full current range plus Insider-exclusive sets. The LEGO Insiders programme is free and earns points on every order, redeemable for free sets. Architecture, Ideas, Technic, Botanical Collection — these adult-targeted lines are LEGO's fastest-growing segments.

Buy first: sets in the Ideas range (fan-designed, often limited-edition). They appreciate in value if kept sealed; the recent Tales of the Space Age and Polaroid OneStep cameras are already trading above retail.

Shop LEGO →
🟫
Lifestyle · Japan

Muji — Japanese minimalism, lived-in

🌐 30+ country sitesLifestyle / stationery

Muji's design philosophy is "no-brand quality goods" — anonymous, considered, functional. The product range spans 7,000+ items: stationery, kitchenware, clothing, furniture, snacks. Cult products include the gel-ink pens, the polypropylene storage boxes, and the aroma diffuser.

Buy first: the gel-ink pen line (0.38mm and 0.5mm) — best disposable pens on Earth, full stop. The Aroma Diffuser and the polypropylene storage system. The skincare line is also unexpectedly good at the price.

Shop Muji →
💎
Discount luxury · UK

The Outnet — Net-a-Porter's discount sibling

🌐 Ships globallyLuxury discount

Owned by Richemont (parent of NAP), The Outnet sells previous-season luxury fashion at 40-75% off original retail. Brands aren't shy — Saint Laurent, Stella McCartney, Khaite, Chloé, The Row, Loewe. Stock is one-of-a-kind by season, so buying decisively pays off.

Buy first: set a saved-search alert for your specific size in your favourite brands. Pieces appear and sell within days. The bi-annual Sale event (June and December) layers another 10-20% on top of the already-discounted prices.

Shop The Outnet →
💼
LVMH luxury · Paris

24S — LVMH's Paris-curated multibrand

🌐 100+ countriesLuxury fashion

LVMH's answer to Net-a-Porter and Mr Porter. 24S curates 300+ brands across women's and men's — Dior, Celine, Loewe, Fendi, plus selected outside designers. Same-as-flagship pricing on LVMH labels but with rare-piece availability you don't always get at brand-direct boutiques.

Buy first: Loewe accessories and Celine ready-to-wear. 24S frequently has size and colourway availability that's already sold out at the brand sites. Twice-yearly sale events drop 30-50% on non-current-season pieces.

Shop 24S →
