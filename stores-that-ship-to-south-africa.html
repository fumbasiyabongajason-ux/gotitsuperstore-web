South African shoppers have never had more access to the world's stores — but the experience is uneven. A few international retailers ship to SA directly and affordably; others charge eye-watering courier fees, or simply won't deliver here at all. And in 2024, SARS removed the old small-parcel concession, so import duties and VAT now apply far more widely than they used to.

This guide sorts the major stores into three groups — ships directly, ships but watch the costs, and needs a freight forwarder — and explains the duty rules in plain language. Every store here is one of the retailers we curate; we link you straight to them.

How import duties work now (the 2024 SARS change, in plain terms)

For years, small online parcels slipped in cheaply: until 31 August 2024, anything valued at R500 or less paid a flat 20% duty and no VAT under a long-standing SARS concession.1 After local retailers raised concerns about unfair competition from the likes of Shein and Temu, SARS scrapped it.4

What that means for you now:

• Since 1 September 2024, 15% import VAT applies on top of the duty — even on low-value parcels — and the old flat rate is being reconfigured to standard rates.13
• Clothing is taxed the hardest — up to 45% import duty plus 15% VAT.2 A “cheap” overseas fashion haul can land far more expensive than the sticker price.
• The courier usually collects duty + VAT before final delivery, which can feel like a surprise bill.

None of this means don’t shop abroad — it means shop with eyes open. For lightweight, low-value or hard-to-find items the maths often still wins; for bulky or heavily-taxed categories like clothing, a local SA store can be cheaper once duty and VAT are added.

"It won't ship to me" — use a freight forwarder

When a store (like Wayfair or Sephora US) doesn't deliver to SA, a freight forwarder gives you a US/UK address, receives your parcel, and forwards it to South Africa. Options South Africans use include Aramex Global Shopper (local and popular), MyUS, and Shop&Ship. You'll pay the forwarding fee plus the usual duties — so it's best for items you genuinely can't get any other way.

Tip: before going the forwarder route, check whether a store we curate already sells the same or a similar item with direct SA delivery — it's usually cheaper and simpler.

Frequently asked questions

Do international stores charge customs duties to South Africa?

Usually, yes. Since SARS removed the small-parcel concession in 2024, most imported orders attract customs duty plus 15% VAT, typically collected by the courier before delivery. Rates vary by product type, with clothing taxed the most.

Does Wayfair ship to South Africa?

Not directly. Wayfair doesn't deliver to South Africa, so you'd need a freight forwarder (such as Aramex Global Shopper or MyUS) that gives you a US address and forwards the parcel — plus the applicable duties.

How do I buy from a store that doesn't ship to South Africa?

Use a freight forwarder: you get a US/UK delivery address, the store ships there, and the forwarder sends it on to you in SA. You'll pay the forwarding fee and import duties, so it's best for items you can't find with direct local delivery.

Is it cheaper to shop internationally or locally in South Africa?

It depends on the item. For lightweight, low-value or hard-to-find products, importing often still wins. For bulky goods or heavily-taxed categories like clothing, a local SA retailer can work out cheaper once duty and VAT are added.
