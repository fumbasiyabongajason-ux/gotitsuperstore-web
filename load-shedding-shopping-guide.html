Shopping when the power goes: a practical guide

South Africa has gone over a year without national load-shedding — a real milestone. But localised outages still hit some areas, so being prepared (and shopping smart) still pays.

If load-shedding shaped how you shopped for years, here's the good news: South Africa passed 300 consecutive days without national load-shedding in 2026, a milestone Eskom credits to its generation-recovery plan.1 By its winter outlook, that run had stretched past 340 days with no load-shedding expected.2

So is preparedness over? Not quite. Localised "load reduction" still affects parts of Gauteng and KwaZulu-Natal, and Eskom doesn't expect to phase it out fully until around 2027.2 Outages from storms and maintenance happen too. A little readiness still goes a long way.

What to keep on hand

For the times the power does drop — planned or not — these are the categories South Africans stock up on:

  • Power banks — keep phones and routers alive.
  • Rechargeable lights & lanterns — safer and brighter than candles.
  • A UPS or small inverter — keeps Wi-Fi, a laptop or the gate motor running.
  • A gas stove or camping burner — hot food and coffee, no electricity needed.
  • Portable solar chargers and battery packs — top up off-grid.
  • Cooler boxes & ice packs — protect fridge/freezer food during longer outages.
Smart move: buy this kit before you need it, online, delivered to your door — rather than hunting through a dim shopping centre when an outage hits and shelves clear.

Why online shopping wins during outages

When the power's out, a mall trip means traffic lights down, queues, card machines offline and wasted petrol. Ordering online and having it delivered sidesteps all of that — your order is picked, packed and brought to you, and you keep your fuel (and your patience). Even with load-shedding largely behind us, the convenience case for delivery is stronger than ever.

Frequently asked questions

Is there still load-shedding in South Africa in 2026?

National load-shedding has been suspended for over a year — South Africa passed 300 consecutive days without it in 2026. However, localised 'load reduction' still affects parts of Gauteng and KwaZulu-Natal, and occasional outages from storms or maintenance still happen, so some preparedness is still wise.

What should I buy to prepare for power outages?

Useful items include power banks, rechargeable lights and lanterns, a UPS or small inverter for Wi-Fi and electronics, a gas or camping stove, portable solar chargers, and cooler boxes with ice packs to protect fridge and freezer food.

Is it better to shop online or in-store during an outage?

Online is usually better. During an outage, malls face downed traffic lights, queues and offline card machines. Ordering online and having it delivered avoids the trip, saves fuel, and means you're not caught in a dark shopping centre.

When will load reduction end in South Africa?

Eskom has been removing customers from load reduction in phases and does not expect to eliminate it fully until around 2027, with large numbers of feeders targeted for removal through 2026.
