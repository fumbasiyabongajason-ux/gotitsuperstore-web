The real fuel cost of driving to the mall

That 'quick' drive to the shops costs more than you think. With petrol at record highs in 2026, it's worth knowing the real rand-per-trip — and when a delivery fee actually beats the fuel.

We rarely count the cost of the trip itself. But in 2026 it adds up fast: from 3 June 2026, inland 95 unleaded petrol hit a record R28.06 per litre — the highest in South African history, surpassing the July 2022 peak.12 Coastal prices sit a little lower, but the direction is the same: every trip is pricier.

What a mall run actually costs

The fuel price is set monthly by the Department of Mineral and Petroleum Resources, so it shifts, but the maths is easy to estimate. At about R28 a litre, a typical petrol car using roughly 8 litres per 100 km costs around R2.20–R2.40 per kilometre in fuel alone.

When delivery is the cheaper choice

Compare like-for-like. If a grocery delivery costs around R36–R453 and your round trip to the shops burns R45+ in fuel — plus parking and an hour of your day — delivery can genuinely be the cheaper, smarter option, especially for a single-purpose trip. Online shopping also curbs impulse buys and lets you compare prices calmly.

Rule of thumb: for a dedicated shopping trip more than ~15–20 km round trip, a delivery fee often costs less than the petrol — and you keep the time.

Frequently asked questions

How much is petrol in South Africa in 2026?

From 3 June 2026, inland 95 unleaded petrol reached a record R28.06 per litre — the highest in South African history. Coastal prices are slightly lower. The price is reviewed monthly by the Department of Mineral and Petroleum Resources, so it changes.

How much does it cost to drive to the mall?

At roughly R28 per litre and about 8 litres per 100 km, fuel costs around R2.20–R2.40 per kilometre. A 20 km round trip is therefore about R45 in petrol alone, before parking, wear and tear, and your time.

Is online delivery cheaper than driving to shop?

Often, yes. If a delivery fee is around R36–R45 and your round trip burns R45 or more in fuel — plus parking and time — delivery can be the cheaper option, especially for a single-purpose shopping trip.

Why is South African fuel so expensive?

The pump price combines the global basic fuel price with levies (the fuel levy, Road Accident Fund levy and others) and margins, set monthly by government. In 2026 these pushed inland 95 petrol to record highs.
