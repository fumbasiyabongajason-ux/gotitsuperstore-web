Is AliExpress safe in South Africa?

AliExpress genuinely ships to South Africa and can be safe — but only if you shop the right way. Here's how to use Buyer Protection, dodge the common traps, and know your real landed cost.

AliExpress is one of the biggest marketplaces on earth and a firm favourite with South African bargain-hunters. The honest answer to "is it safe?" is: yes, if you shop smart. It's a marketplace of many independent sellers, so safety comes down to choosing carefully and using the protections built into the platform.

Does AliExpress ship to South Africa?

Yes — AliExpress ships to South Africa, with a range of courier options. The cheapest untracked post can take many weeks and is easier to lose; a tracked courier option costs a little more but is far safer and faster. Delivery times vary widely by seller and shipping method.

How to stay safe on AliExpress

• Use Buyer Protection. AliExpress holds your payment until you confirm delivery, and offers refunds if an item doesn't arrive or doesn't match the listing — never pay a seller outside the platform.
• Check the seller's rating and reviews (and photo reviews) before buying. High ratings and lots of orders are a good sign.
• Choose tracked shipping so a parcel can't quietly vanish.
• Pay with a card that supports 3D Secure / OTP for an extra layer.
• If a price seems impossible, be cautious — counterfeits and "too good to be true" listings exist.

Tip: open a dispute before the Buyer Protection timer runs out if something's wrong. Once you click "confirm received," it's much harder to get your money back.

Don't forget duties

Since SARS removed the small-parcel concession in 2024, imported parcels — including AliExpress orders — generally attract customs duty plus 15% VAT, usually collected by the courier before delivery.1 Factor that into the price, especially on larger orders.

Frequently asked questions

Is AliExpress safe for South African buyers?

Yes, if you shop smart: buy from highly-rated sellers, keep payment on-platform to use Buyer Protection, choose tracked shipping, and pay with a 3D Secure card. AliExpress holds your money until you confirm delivery and offers refunds for items that don't arrive or don't match.

Does AliExpress deliver to South Africa?

Yes. AliExpress ships to South Africa with several courier options. Pick a tracked option rather than the cheapest untracked post, which is slower and easier to lose. Delivery times vary by seller and method.

Do I pay customs on AliExpress orders to South Africa?

Usually yes. Since the 2024 SARS change, imported parcels generally attract customs duty plus 15% VAT, typically collected by the courier before delivery. Add this to your budget, especially on bigger orders.

How do I get a refund on AliExpress?

Open a dispute through AliExpress before the Buyer Protection period ends if an item doesn't arrive or isn't as described. Don't click 'confirm received' until you're satisfied, as that closes your protection window.
