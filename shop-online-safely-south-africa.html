How to shop online safely in South Africa

Online shopping is convenient — and a magnet for fraud. South African banking fraud runs into billions a year. Here's how to protect your money and your data, in plain language.

South Africans lost billions of rand to financial crime in recent years, with digital banking and card-not-present (online) fraud among the fastest-growing categories.2 The good news: a handful of habits stop the vast majority of scams. Here's how to shop online with confidence.

Eight habits that keep you safe

1. Buy from reputable, known retailers. Stick to established stores (or a curated marketplace that vets them). If you've never heard of a site offering an unbelievable deal, be suspicious.
2. Check for a secure connection. Look for https and a padlock, and type the address yourself rather than following links from messages.
3. Use 3D Secure / OTP. Cards that prompt a one-time PIN for online purchases add a real layer of protection — enable it with your bank.1
4. Never share your OTP or PIN. No legitimate bank or store will ever ask for it. Criminals who have your password still need that code.1
5. Beware phishing. Fake emails and SMSes that mimic banks or couriers want you to click a link and "log in." Don't — go to the official app or site directly.1
6. Guard against SIM-swap fraud. If your phone suddenly loses signal for no reason, contact your network — criminals use SIM swaps to intercept OTPs.1
7. Use a single card (or virtual card) for online spend. It limits exposure and makes dodgy transactions easy to spot.
8. Watch your statements and turn on transaction alerts so you catch anything unfamiliar fast.

⚠️ The newer traps: scammers now use fake "3D Secure" pop-ups, QR-code redirects and even AI-generated emails. If a payment step looks unusual or rushes you, stop and verify through your banking app directly.

Know your rights: POPIA

South Africa's Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA), in full effect since 1 July 2021, gives you enforceable rights over your personal data.3 Legitimate retailers must collect only what they need, tell you how they'll use it, keep it secure, and not sell it without consent. If a store is vague about what it does with your data, that's a red flag. Concerns can be raised with the Information Regulator.

Frequently asked questions

How do I know an online store is safe in South Africa?

Stick to established, reputable retailers (or a curated marketplace that vets them), check for https and a padlock, type the web address yourself, and be wary of unknown sites with unbelievable deals. Use a card with 3D Secure and turn on transaction alerts.

What is 3D Secure and should I use it?

3D Secure adds a one-time PIN (OTP) step to online card payments, confirming it's really you. Yes — enable it with your bank. It's one of the most effective protections against card-not-present fraud, which is a major share of South African card-fraud losses.

Should I ever share my OTP or banking PIN?

Never. No legitimate bank, retailer or courier will ask for your OTP, PIN or password. Anyone who does is a fraudster, even if they know some of your details already.

What rights do I have under POPIA?

POPIA gives you control over your personal information: businesses must collect only what they need, be transparent about how they use it, keep it secure, and not sell it without consent. You can complain to South Africa's Information Regulator if a company mishandles your data.
